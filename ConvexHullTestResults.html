Observations:

In all test cases, the shortest time is taken by Graham Scan, followed by Jarvis March and Kirkpatrick Seidel is the slowest algorithm (in terms of time taken).

Even though the worst case complexity of Kirkpatrick Seidel is O(NlogN) (where N is the number of points given), which is better than that of Jarvis March, O(N x E) (E is the number of points in convex hull, worst case O(N)), the time taken for jarvis march is much lower than that of Kirkpatrick Seidel.

Analysis: 

The above observation can the following explanation, We have used the vector data structure for implementation. The calculation of median in Kirkpatrick Seidel uses time expensive operations of vector much more than the other two algorithms.

The overall complexity of the algorithm does not indicate how much time will it take for a particular input size, but indicates how will that time taken grow as a function of input size. O(NlogN) or O(N x E) is just an order of time taken. We cannot deduce which algorithm will take least time by just looking at their overall complexities. Overall time depends on actual number of operations performed and complexity indicates the order of that number (not the actual number).
